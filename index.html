Projects in Digital Composing Portfolio

Phase 1: Exploring Digital Media


P1: Self Introduction

Project Fuego allows us to introduce ourselves while displaying our strength and interests

P2: Apple Tree

Apple Tree is a website that simulates the experience of illustration books. The pictures are connected to tell a narrative of apple tree growth.

P3: Going Home

Going Home is a short game about a traveler going back home. The player has to select the right choice to keep the traveler safe.

Phase 1 Course Goals


1. Foster the creative habits of mind

The projects encourages me to think outside the box based on my interests. We were able to introduce our hobbies and skills in the Fuego project, allowing the gears to turn. For instance, illustration is a skill that I currently possessed; Hence, Apple Tree is solely based on visual. However, I want to have a more meaningful experience than just compiling a bunch of pictures together. An creative idea I came up with was formating the website like a book, as to me, books fits the definition of art. The projects also foster a good creative habits by being selective and reasonable within time limit. I had a lot of ambitious ideas in mind before the creation process. Having a balance between ones' visions and realism is important in the real world. Practricing wireframing in Going Home helps me plan out the whole project, as well as what is possible or not.

2. Engage with others in a workshop environment.

Within classes, we were given workshop time to fix any bugs and see other classmate's project. As someone who had a bit of prior experience with CSS and HTML, I was able to help others with simple instructions such as changing background colors and overseeing minor mistakes. Seeing other's project encourages to incorporate ideas into my own future projects. I also love how everyone's websites display their own personality. I learned how to debug my codes since I saw people having issues with their website not loading. Basically, learning by watching others trying to solve their mistakes.

3. Think deeply about craft.

Each projects are given different purposes and functions. For instance, Making 1 is meant to be more simple and personal. Making 2 focuses on making a single html page that fits our definition of art. Lastly, Making 3 wants us to use multiple html to make webtext designs that are either linear or non-linear. I had think carefully about selecting a subject that follows under the limit of what is given. For example, a game like Going Home would not exactly work with a single HTML page as it can easily ruin the surprise that comes with the consequences of the choices. Wireframing in the third makings helped me a lot of structuring the websites and craft at its full potentials; It helps with organization as I have a total of 14 html pages.

4. Engage in sustained research and skills development.

We were permitted to search outside sources for materials and tutorials to enhance our websites. For me, I did most of my research for Apple Tree. Although I have touched Javascript, I had no experience when it comes to making parallax scrolls. After several Youtube tutorials, I was able to successfully make it using similar codes and some adjustments. The readings provided during class are usually related to the project that we are currently on, giving us a lot of new ideas and approaches. Wireframing is a skill that is widely used during website and UI designs, making a great practice for real life jobs.

5. Learn to present your work

We are given the chance to present our work to the class. Presenting our works allows me to learn new techniques widen my creativity. It can also convey ourselves in a meaningful way by displaying our skills or the messages we want to send out. Additionally, receiving criticism and feedback from others will give us a new perspective. I presented twice during the projects, making minor adjustments throughout my codes such as spotting spelling mistakes and fixing a button in the third making. I doublechecked my alt-tags and letter sizing to make sure it can be accessible to anyone. My images are perfectly set to 100% to ensure a size that can be scene and doesn't look funky on screen.
